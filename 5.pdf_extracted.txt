1. Word Meanings
1.1 Denotation and Reference
Denotation: the relationship that holds between that lexeme and persons, things, places,
properties, processes and activities external to the language system.
外延是指一个语言单位（特别是词项）与其所指的非语言实体间的关系（即语言表达与
其在语言系统之外的现实世界中所指事物之间的关系），因此相当于指称义。
Reference: the relationship that holds between an expression and what that expression stands
for on particular occasions of its utterance.
reference 指语言表达与具体现象之间的关系，即具体语境中的语言表达与外部事物间的
关系。
1.2 Denotation and Sense
Sense: the relationship between the words or expressions of a single language. It denotes the
relationship inside the language. The sense of an expression is its place in a system of semantic
relationships with other expressions in the language.
涵义是指语言内部的关系系统，即语言内部一个词项与其他词项相联系。涵义独立于语
言表达与指称对象间的指称关系。
Every word that has meaning has sense but not every word has denotation; besides, the
expressions with different senses may have the same denotation.
e.g., There is no such animal as a unicorn.
Morning Star is the Evening Star.
Aristotle is the teacher of Alexander.
2. Motivation
 How do words get meaning?
2.1 Conventionality:
Most English words are conventional, arbitrary symbols. There is no connection between the
word form and its meaning.
2.2 Motivation:
Some English words are motivated symbols. There is some connection between the word
form and its meaning.
 Onomatopoeic motivation
Words motivated phonetically are called echoic or onomatopoeic words, whose pronunciation

 Morphologic motivation
Morphologic motivation means that the meaning of some words can be inferred from the
meaning of the affixes, roots, or other parts of the words.
 Semantic motivation
It refers to the mental associations suggested by the conceptual meaning of a word. It explains
the connection between the literal sense and figurative sense of the word.
 Etymologic motivation
The origins of words throw light on their meanings. All the words commonized from proper
names can be explained in terms of their origins（专有名词普通化）.

 Number analogy
First family, first lady, the Fourth world
 Place and space analogy

3. Types of Meaning
3.1 Conceptual Meaning（概念意义）
Conceptual meaning (also called denotative or cognitive meaning): is meaning given in the
dictionary and forms the core of word meaning. 概念意义是一个词的中心意义或核心意义，它直
接、明确地表示所指对象，因此又被称为“外延意义”。
3.2 Associative meaning（联想意义）
Associative meaning: is the secondary meaning supplemented to the conceptual meaning.
联想意义是指词汇的附带意义，与概念意义一起构成词汇意义。
Associative meaning can be reflected by connotative meaning, affective meaning, collocative
meaning and social meaning.

3.2.1 Connotative Meaning（内涵意义）
Connotative Meaning: the communicative value of an expression by virtue of what it refers to,
over and above its purely conceptual content, or the additional meaning of the conceptual meaning.
Connotative Meaning is not an essential part of the word meaning, but social and cultural values
that might occur in the mind of particular users of the language. 内涵意义是指通过语言所指事物
来传递的意义，是除“概念意义”以外的附加意义，因此具有交际的价值。
3.2.2 Social or Stylistic Meaning（社会意义）
Social meaning refers to the kind of meaning a piece of language conveys about the social
circumstance of its use.
From the use of words we can recognize the geographical, social origin of the speaker, or the
social relationship between the speaker and hearer. Besides, words vary in style, usually ranging on
a continuum from casual or colloquial to formal or polite according to the type of communicative
situation.
3.2.3 Affective Meaning（情感意义）
Affective meaning expresses the speaker’s or the writer’s personal feelings and attitudes.
In English, some words can express the speakers’ approval or disapproval of the persons or
things he is talking about.
 Words that show speakers’ approval are purr words (appreciative term).
 Words that show speakers’ disapproval are snarl words (pejorative term).
3.2.4 Reflected Meaning（反映意义）
Reflected meaning is the meaning which arises in cases of multiple conceptual meanings,
when one sense of a word forms part of our response to another sense. It is the product of people’s
recognition and imagination. 反映意义是指能引起听者或读者联想的意义，即“当一个词的一
种意义构成我们对这个词的另一种意义的反应的一部分时，便产生反映意义”。
3.2.5 Collocative Meaning（搭配意义）
Collocative meaning consists of the associations a word acquires on account of the meaning of
words which tend to occur in its environment. In other words, it is the part of the word meaning
suggested by the words with which it co-occurs.
3.3 Thematic Meaning（主题意义）
Thematic Meaning: the meaning arising out of the way in which the writer or speaker
organizes his message, in terms of sentence sequence, emphasis, focus of information.
主题意义是指说话者或作者借助组织信息的方式（语序、强调手段、信息焦点的安排）来传
递的一种意义。

4. Analysis of Word Meanings
4.1 Semantic Field（语义场）:
Semantic Field Theory 又称作lexical field theory（词汇场理论），是德国结构主义语言学家
J. Trier 提出。
 The theory is concerned with the vocabulary of a language as a system of interrelated lexical
networks.
 Semantic field theory took the view that the vocabulary of a given language is not simply a
listing of independent items, but is organized into areas, or fields, within which words interrelated
and define each other in various ways.
语义场是指在所指层面上，把一群有某一共同语义特征的词汇单位聚集在一块而组成的
一组词，他们相互界定语义特征，也与其他义场的边沿成分区别开来。
4.2 Componential Analysis（语义成分分析法）:
In order to conduct a closer analysis of word meanings, some linguists segment the
composition of word meaning, trying to find some common semantic components for the analysis.
From this, a semantic theory is formulated that all lexical items can be analyzed into a set of
semantic features or semantic components which may be universal. This is termed Componential
Analysis (CA), a process of breaking down the sense of a word into its minimal components (Leech
1981).
