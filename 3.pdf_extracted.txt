Chapter 3 Morphological Structure of English
1. Introduction:
 Language can be defined as “an arbitrary vocal system used for human communication”. By
system we mean that the elements in a language are arranged according to certain rules, but not
at random.
 Language presents itself as a hierarchy in different aspects. Elements at a lower level are thus
combined according to certain rules to form elements at a higher level. As is shown in the
following hierarchical rank scale, language rises from morpheme at the bottom up to the
sentence at the top in terms of lexicography.
2. Morpheme, Lexeme and Morph:
2.1 Morpheme
2.1.1 Is a Word the Most Basic Unit of Meaning?
Words are usually treated as the basic and minimal units of a language to make sentences,
which are combinations of words according to syntactic rules. But is a word the most basic unit of
meaning?
Words are not the basic meaningful elements of a language, since some words contain several
distinct units of meaning. The linguistic term for the most elemental unit of grammatical form is
morpheme. The word is derived from the Greek word morphe, meaning “form”.
2.1.2 What is Morpheme?
Syntactically, “morpheme is the ultimate (the most basic/elementary) grammatical constituent,
the smallest meaningful unit of language”. In other words, a morpheme is the minimal form of
grammatical analysis.
In view of word-formation, morpheme can be defined as “the smallest functioning unit in the
composition of words, not divisible or analyzable into smaller forms” (Crystal 1985). 词素是构词
时起作用的最小单位。
Morpheme vs. Word:
Morphemes occur in speech only as constituent parts of words. They can not be used
independently, although a word may consist of a single morpheme. Nor are morphemes divisible

Morpheme vs. Syllable:
The word lady can be divided into two syllables (la‧dy), but it consists of just one morpheme,

2.2 Types of Morphemes:
2.2.1 Free Morphemes and Bound Morphemes
Let us see an example. The word reader can be divided into read and –er. However, read
cannot be split into smaller morphemes. This means that the word read is itself a single morpheme.
To put it simple, a morpheme which can stand alone as a word is called a free morpheme. By
contrast, -er has to combine with other morphemes. So it is a bound morpheme.
Free morphemes: are the morphemes which are independent of other morphemes. That is to
say, a free morpheme need not be attached to other morphemes; it is one that may constitute a word
(free form) by itself. Free morphemes have complete meanings in themselves and can be used as
free grammatical units in sentences.
自由词素是具有完整的词汇意义，能够单独构成一个词的词素，而且可以作为自由的语
法单位独立使用。因此，在这一意义上，自由词素等同于词。
Bound morphemes: are the morphemes that can not occur as separate words. They are so
named because they are bound (attached) to other morphemes to form words or to perform a
particular grammatical function; they must appear with at least one other morpheme, free or bound.
Unlike free morphemes, they do not have independent semantic meaning; instead, they have
attached meaning or grammatical meaning. 不具有独立意义，只具有附加意义或语法意义。
2.2.2 Derivational Morphemes and Inflectional Morphemes
Derivational morphemes: are morphemes used to derive new words. In English, derivatives
and compounds are all formed by such morphemes. A derivational morpheme can change the
meaning or the word class, or both the meaning and word class of a word; many derivational
morphemes have a specific lexical meaning. It can be both prefixes and suffixes.
Inflectional morphemes: in contrast, are morphemes used to indicate the syntactic
relationships between words and function as grammatical markers.
The inflectional morpheme does not form a new word with a new lexical meaning when it is
added to another word. Nor does it change the word-class of the word to which it is affixed. It just
adds some grammatical information to the word. Inflectional morphemes are confined to suffixes.

2.2.3 Root, Affix, Stem, Base
Alternatively, morphemes may be also divided into roots (or root morphemes) and affixes (or
affixational morphemes).
Affix（词缀）:
Affixes are morphemes which only occur when attached to other morphemes. By definition
affixes are bound morphemes.
There are three types of affixes: prefix, suffix and infix. A prefix is an affix attached before a
root, and a suffix after a root. An infix is an affix inserted into the root itself. Infixing is very rare in
English; the only infix that occurs in English morphology is -m- which is inserted before the last
consonant of the root in a few words of Latin origin.
Root:
As defined by Bauer:
“A root is a form which is not further analyzable, either in terms of derivational or inflectional
morphology. It is that part of a word form that remains when all the inflectional and derivational
affixes have been removed. A root is the basic part always present in a lexeme.” (Bauer 1983:20)
A root is the basic unchangeable part of a word, and covers the main lexical meaning of the
word. That is to say, a root is the part of the word left, whether free or bound, when all the
inflectional and derivational affixes are removed. It carries the main component of meaning in a
word. Roots are, therefore, the cores of English words.
Historically, the root is the earliest form of a word. Roots are either free or bound:
Free and Bound Root:
All morphemes are bound or free. Affixes are bound morphemes. Root morphemes, can be
bound or free.

Stem:
Bauer defines “stem” as follows:
“A stem is of concern only when dealing with inflectional morphology. ... Only Inflectional (but
not derivational) affixes are added to it: it is the part of the word form which remains when all the
inflectional affixes have been removed.” A stem is any morpheme or combination of morphemes to
which an inflectional affix can be added. 词干是指一个词去掉其所有屈折词缀后剩下的部分。
Base:
A base is a lexical item to which affixes of any kind (including both derivational and inflectional)
can be added. It may also be defined as “a form to which a rule of word formation is applied”.
词基是指一个词内可以对其进行操作的任一单位。
2.2.4 Content and Grammatical Morphemes
On a semantic and syntactic basis, morphemes can fall into content and grammatical
morphemes.
Content morphemes are lexical morphemes used to derive new words, so also known as
derivational morphemes (free or bound, with lexical content).
Grammatical morphemes function primarily as grammatical markers (inflectional affixes,
and free morphemes such as in, and, do, have, they, while, where, but and that, functional words).

2.3 Morph and Allomorph
2.3.1 Morph（形素）
Any concrete realization of a morpheme in a given utterance is called a morph, such as cat,
chair, -ing, -s, etc. Morphemes are abstract units, which are realized in speech by discrete units,
known as morphs, a physical form. Morphs are actually spoken, minimal carriers of meaning. 形素
是词素在言语中的具体实现。词素是抽象单位，在言语中实现为一些离散单位，即形素（具
体的形式）。
2.3.2 Allomorph（语素/词素变体）
Most morphemes are realized in speech by single morphs like bird, tree, green, desire, want,
etc. Some morphemes, however, are realized by more than one morph, depending on the context in
which they occur. A morpheme may have different morphological forms or phonetic forms in
different contexts. 指同一个语素的各种形式，即语素在实际使用时的体现。有些语素在不同的
语境中体现为不同的语素形式。
Allomorphs are the alternate morphological and phonetic forms of the same morpheme, for
example, [t], [d] and [id] are allomorphs of the past tense morpheme in English.
2.4 Lexeme, Word Form, Lexical Entry (Optional)
2.4.1 Lexeme
Lexeme is also an important concept in lexicology.
 Lexeme is a basic unit of lexical meaning, an abstract linguistic unit with different
variants, for example, sing as against sang, sung. 词位指一种语言意义体系中词汇的基
本单位，是拥有不同变体的一种抽象的语言单位。
1) Why it is abstract?
Lexeme is a word in the sense of an item of vocabulary that can be listed in the ideal dictionary.
It is an abstract vocabulary item, and doesn’t exist in the language use.
2) What does a lexical unit with different variants mean?
Lexeme is an abstract vocabulary item with a common core of meaning, and can be realized by
different word forms. Put it in another way, lexeme is a set of linguistic signs which share the same
lexical meanings but different in their grammatical meanings.
3) What does a linguistic unit mean?
Lexeme exists regardless of any inflectional endings it may have or the number of words it
may contain. Some set expressions would be each considered a single lexeme.

2.4.2 Word Form
Word form is the realization (representation or manifestation) of the lexeme. Equivalently, it is
the inflected forms of a lexeme.
