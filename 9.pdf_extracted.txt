## 第 4 页
Semantic Unity
Each idiom is a single semantic unity. The meaning

of an idiom is opaque; i.e. the meaning of an idiom is
not the total sum of the meaning of its constituents.
till the cows come home:

for a very long time; forever
The semantic unity of idioms is also reflected in the

illogical relationship between the literal meaning of
each constituent and the meaning of the idiom.
How do you do?


## 第 16 页
Rhetorical Features of Idioms
Apart from the stylistic features, idioms can bring about certain

rhetorical effects in terms of sound and meaning.
(1) Phonetic manipulation（音韵手段）
a. alliteration such as “neither fish, flesh, nor fowl”

b. rhyme such as “toil and moil”

c. reverse rhyme such as “part and parcel”

(2) Lexical manipulation（词语手段）
a. Reiteration, which means the duplication of synonyms

such as “pick and choose”（同义词迭用）.
b. Repetition, which means the repetition of the same word,

such as “by and by”（词语重复）.
c. Juxtaposition, which means the combination of two

antonyms such as “day and night”（反义词并置）.
